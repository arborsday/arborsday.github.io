Co-Founders with Rich Backgrounds

Opay was founded in 2018 by Yahui Zhou, who previously co-founded Interswitch Group and served as the CEO of Opera Software. Other co-founders include Dmitry Shkolnik, who served as the CEO of AIG Russia and Central Asia, and Chamath Palihapitiya, a venture capitalist and the former Vice President of User Growth at Facebook.

Fast-growing Nigerian Fintech

Opay quickly established itself as a major player in the Nigerian fintech scene, offering a variety of services including mobile payments, food delivery, transportation, and more. In just two years, the company has raised over $1.5 billion in funding and claims to have millions of users in Nigeria.

One-Stop Shop for Daily Needs

Opay's business model is centered around providing a convenient one-stop shop for users to access various daily needs such as payments, food delivery, transportation, and other services. The company makes money by taking a percentage of transactions made through its platform, as well as through advertising and other revenue streams.

Capitalizing on Unbanked Population

Opay aims to provide financial services to Nigeria's largely unbanked population through its mobile platform. By offering a wide range of services, Opay is looking to increase financial inclusion in Nigeria and tap into the massive potential of the country's growing middle class.

Major Funding History of Opay

Opay has raised over $1.5 billion in funding to date, with investors including IDG Capital, Sequoia Capital China, Meituan-Dianping, and the Norwegian telecom operator Telenor Group. These investments have helped the company rapidly scale its operations and expand its services in Nigeria and beyond.
