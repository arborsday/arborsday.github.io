The Airwallex Trio

Airwallex was founded in Melbourne, Australia in 2015 by Jack Zhang, Lucy Liu and Max Li. They were inspired to create the company after encountering cross-border payment difficulties while running their own e-commerce business.

From Aussie Startup to Global Powerhouse

Airwallex has quickly become one of the leading players in the digital cross-border payments industry. The company has raised over $360 million in funding, with its most recent valuation reaching $2 billion. Airwallex provides cross-border payments, foreign exchange, and digital wallets to businesses and individuals in over 130 countries.

Simplifying Cross-Border Payments

Airwallex's business model is focused on simplifying cross-border payments for businesses and individuals. The company provides its clients with access to its own proprietary platform that offers real-time foreign exchange rates and enables users to make cross-border payments at a fraction of the cost of traditional methods. Airwallex generates revenue through a combination of transaction fees, forex markup, and interest earned on customer deposits.

The Future of Cross-Border Payments

Airwallex's vision is to create a world without financial borders, making cross-border payments as easy and accessible as domestic payments. With its innovative technology, Airwallex is well-positioned to lead the charge in this industry and continue to disrupt traditional cross-border payment methods.

Major Funding History of Airwallex

Since its founding, Airwallex has attracted significant investment from a wide range of investors, including Sequoia Capital, Hillhouse Capital, and DST Global. In 2020, the company raised $160 million in a funding round led by Sequoia Capital, valuing the company at over $1 billion and making it one of Australia's first unicorns.
