A Trio of Friends with a Vision

The founder of Vinted, Milda Mitkute, Mantas Mikuckas and Justas Janauskas, were friends from Lithuania who saw a gap in the market for a second-hand clothing platform. They launched Vinted in 2008.

A Resale Revolution

Vinted has become one of the largest second-hand clothing marketplaces in Europe, with over 40 million members in 13 countries. The platform offers a safe and easy way for people to buy and sell second-hand clothing, making sustainable fashion accessible to everyone.

The Resale Rundown

Vinted operates as a marketplace, connecting buyers and sellers of second-hand clothing. The company makes money through commissions on sales and advertising. In addition, Vinted offers paid features such as premium memberships and a shipping label printing service.

The Future is Second-Hand

Vinted's success shows the growing demand for sustainable and affordable fashion. The platform not only provides a platform for people to buy and sell second-hand clothing, but also promotes a circular economy and reducing waste.

Major Funding History of Vinted

Vinted has received multiple rounds of funding, with the most recent being a $229 million Series D in 2020. The company has raised a total of $318 million to date, with investors including Accel, Insight Partners, and Burda Principal Investments.
